Logistics: drove own car from Florida with ALL THREE BICYCLES · Hotel: The

Hotel report — The Ann Savannah: two-story loft · pool access · very nice
gym · full kitchen in loft · Little James restaurant (verified name):
fantastic coffee + food, all three meals · lounge space for checkers &
cards. Pre-arrival email win: incidentals removed for Seth + early
birthday amenity offered (mini chocolate cake/cookies — Kayla, front desk).

Meals: Breakfast/coffee — Little James at The Ann · Lunch — Brighter Day
Natural Foods deli (Bull St; verified) + The Collins Quarter at Forsyth
(excellent, long wait — check in on arrival) · Dinner — riverfront spots
behind Plant Riverside (everything good) + ice cream shops.

Do again (all FREE except the riverboat):
1. Forsyth Park & Farmers' Market — Sat 9–1 — THE must-go; best farmers
   market Elizabeth has ever been to (breads, pecans, pastries, local art —
   Seth's artwork); towels under the oaks on dry days. 2–3 hrs.
2. The Collins Quarter at Forsyth — café in the park, 1 hr.
3. Brighter Day Natural Foods — deli/grocery at park edge, 30 min.
4. Hotel Bardo — stroll/coffee/cool-off; great spa (Elizabeth stays on
   business trips). 30–45 min.
5. Historic squares by bike — Elizabeth's running route, 1–2 hrs.
6. Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Baptist — so pretty, 30 min.
7. Plant Riverside District — converted power plant JW Marriott; walk
   through + riverfront food out back; 2–3 hrs.
8. River Street & riverfront walk — food + ice cream, 1–2 hrs.
9. Savannah Riverboat Cruise — short loop is right-sized; online coupons.
10. Starland Yard — live music/food/shopping; Seth loved the pizza. 1 hr.

Banked: Electric Moon rooftop (Plant Riverside) Sunday brunch w/ DJ.

Markets: Forsyth Farmers' Market, Sat 9–1 — the best one yet.
