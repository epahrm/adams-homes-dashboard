Philosophy that worked: avoided trying to "see everything"; focused on
high-impact experiences. Balance of SF icons + Silicon Valley + World Cup.

## Tuesday — San Francisco icons

- ✅ **Battery Spencer** — best Golden Gate viewpoint, classic postcard
  photos, top photography location of the trip. Do again: absolutely.
- ✅ **Pier 39** — sea lions, waterfront, birthday atmosphere for Seth. No SF
  trip with a child is complete without the sea lions. Worth every minute.
- ✅ **Ferry Building Marketplace** — local food, Bay Bridge, Embarcadero
  walk. Chosen over fighting downtown traffic.
- ❌ **Palace of Fine Arts** — originally planned; cut for heavy traffic,
  time constraints, and already having exceptional bridge photos. Correct.
- ❌ **Sausalito** — Battery Spencer + Pier 39 + Ferry Building was the
  stronger combination.
- ❌ **Lombard Street** — traffic not worth the payoff on a short trip. No
  regrets.

## Wednesday morning — Silicon Valley

- ✅ **Apple Park Visitor Center** — rooftop, merch, Silicon Valley
  experience. Worth doing once.
- ✅ **Santana Row** — beautiful atmosphere, excellent walking, great
  restaurants. Perfect before the World Cup.
- ✅ **San Pedro Square Market** — best last-minute addition: World Cup
  atmosphere, soccer celebration, food hall, giant screens, fan energy.
  Hidden gem of the trip.

## FIFA World Cup — Levi's Stadium (centerpiece)

Arrive early · experience fan zones · walk the entire concourse · stay after
the match if possible.

## Researched but skipped (with reasons)

- Palace of Fine Arts — routing. Good decision.
- Winchester Mystery House — Seth wasn't interested. Correct.
- Municipal Rose Garden — beautiful, not the right fit.
- Hakone Gardens — excellent; recommend for a future adults trip.
- Communications Hill — not strong enough vs World Cup atmosphere.
- Stanford University — next-best stop if there were another half day.
- PayPal Park — no meaningful pregame festivities; San Pedro Square was
  the right call.
